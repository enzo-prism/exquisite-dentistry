## When to Get Veneers for The Four Front Teeth

While extending veneer application to all prominently visible teeth is recommended for a seamless aesthetic, your cosmetic dentist might suggest concentrating solely on the front four teeth for particular reasons. This focused strategy can effectively address minor chips, cracks, or gaps between teeth and enhance bite alignment. However, applying only four veneers to cut costs is not advisable. Your smile deserves the best; compromising quality can detract from its overall appearance and longevity. For those in the Los Angeles area, Dr. Alexie Aguil can guide you in determining the optimal number and placement of veneers for your individual needs.

### Comprehensive Veneer Treatment

Dr. Aguil suggests treating 8 to 12 teeth per arch for a harmonious smile design. This approach accounts for factors such as overall bite alignment and tooth color consistency, ensuring a natural-looking result. If you look for a less expensive option to treat fewer teeth or use lower-quality veneers, you may have more issues that can be costly to address later on. Lower-quality veneers may not give you the long-term result you were expecting. Investing in high-quality porcelain veneers for most of your smile helps prevent future issues and additional expenses associated with inferior materials.
