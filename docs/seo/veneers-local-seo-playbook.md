## 1. Google Business Profile optimization

The GBP is the single highest-leverage asset for these terms. Proximity, completeness, and reviews are what the map pack rewards. Work through this list and leave nothing blank.

### Categories
- **Primary category:** `Cosmetic dentist`. This is the most important single setting for veneers intent. Confirm it is primary, not secondary.
- **Secondary categories** (add all that genuinely apply):
  - `Dentist`
  - `Dental clinic`
  - `Teeth whitening service`
  - `Denture care center` (only if restorative dentures are offered)
  - `Emergency dental service` (only if the practice actually takes emergencies)
- Do not pad with categories the practice does not serve. Irrelevant categories dilute relevance and can trigger quality flags.

### Services
Add each of the following as a **named service** under the Cosmetic dentist category, with a short description in the practice voice. Keep descriptions plain and specific.

- **Porcelain veneers** — "Thin porcelain shells bonded to the front of the teeth to adjust shape, color, and alignment. Planned tooth by tooth with Dr. Aguil."
- **Dental veneers** — "Custom veneers to refine the look of the smile. We review options, fit, and care so you know what to expect before we begin."
- **Composite veneers** — "Tooth-colored composite shaped directly onto the teeth in a single visit. A more conservative option we discuss case by case."
- **Smile makeover** — "A coordinated plan that may combine veneers, whitening, and restorative work to address several teeth together. We start with a consultation and a clear sequence."

Add the practice's other real services as well (whitening, crowns, bonding, cleanings, exams). A fuller services list strengthens overall category relevance.

### Attributes
Set every attribute that is accurate. Common useful ones for this practice:
- Accessibility: wheelchair-accessible entrance / restroom / parking, as applicable.
- Amenities: restroom, free or paid parking, Wi-Fi, gender-neutral restroom — only what is true.
- Planning: appointment required, accepts new patients, online care/appointments.
- Payments: accepted methods, and financing if offered (the practice offers Cherry financing — reflect that if an attribute fits).

### Business description
Use the 750-character field. Mention veneers and the Mid-Wilshire / Beverly Hills-adjacent location naturally, in voice. Draft:

> Exquisite Dentistry is the cosmetic and restorative practice of Dr. Alexie Aguil on Wilshire Boulevard in Mid-Wilshire, Los Angeles, a short drive from Beverly Hills, West Hollywood, and Mid-City. Each veneer case is planned tooth by tooth, with time to review options, fit, and cost before we begin. New patients are welcome. Call (323) 272-2388 to arrange a consultation.

Keep it under the character limit. Do not add links inside the description (Google strips them and it reads as spam).

### Hours
- Enter regular hours exactly as the practice operates.
- Set **special hours** for holidays in advance. A profile showing wrong holiday hours loses clicks and erodes trust signals.

### Booking and action links
- Add the **website** (homepage or a dedicated veneers/consultation page).
- Add a **booking link** (the practice's scheduler or a consultation-request page).
- Add the **phone** exactly as the standardized NAP.

### NAP consistency
The name, address, and phone on the GBP must match the website and schema **character for character**. See Section 4. Do not add neighborhood keywords or taglines to the business **name** field — name-stuffing violates Google guidelines and risks suspension. The legal/operating name only.

## 2. Photo and GBP-post strategy

Photos and Posts keep the profile active and give veneers cases a place to show up visually.

### Before/after veneer photos
- Only publish cases where you hold **documented, signed patient consent** for marketing use. Keep the consent form on file. When in doubt, do not post.
- Show honest results. Google prohibits **misleading edits** — no whitening boosts, no reshaping, no filters that change the clinical outcome. Match lighting and angle between before and after so the comparison is fair.
- Caption plainly and without claims about specific outcomes: "Porcelain veneers, upper front teeth." Avoid "best," "perfect," or guarantees.
- Never include a patient's name or any detail tied to identity.

### Interior, exterior, and team photos
- **Exterior:** the building and entrance on Wilshire, so patients recognize it from the street. Reinforces location relevance.
- **Interior:** reception, operatories, and any technology patients will see.
- **Team:** Dr. Aguil and staff, plain and approachable.
- Refresh a few photos each month rather than uploading everything once.

### GBP Posts cadence
- Post **every 1–2 weeks**. Consistency matters more than volume.
- Rotate post types: an update (veneers planning, whitening, a new service), an offer (only if genuine), a photo highlight (a consented before/after or interior shot).
- Keep copy in voice. Example update post:

  > Considering veneers? We plan each case tooth by tooth and review fit, color, and cost before any work begins. Call (323) 272-2388 to arrange a consultation in Mid-Wilshire.

### Geo and seasonal angles
- **Geo:** reference Mid-Wilshire, Beverly Hills-adjacent, West Hollywood, and Mid-City naturally across posts over time — not stuffed into one.
- **Seasonal:** weddings and graduations in spring/summer, year-end and holidays in late fall. Frame calmly: "Planning a smile for a spring event? Veneer cases take time to plan — earlier consultations leave room to do it carefully."

## 3. Review velocity and quality

Reviews are the second-biggest pack lever after proximity. The aim is a **steady flow** of genuine Google reviews, some of which naturally mention "veneers" by name.

### Hard rules (Google policy and law)
- **Do not incentivize.** No discounts, gift cards, raffles, or anything of value for a review.
- **Do not gate.** Do not screen for happy patients first and route only them to Google ("review gating"). Ask everyone in the eligible group the same way.
- **Never write or stage reviews**, and do not ask staff or family to.
- **HIPAA:** a patient may say anything about their own care, but the practice must never confirm, deny, or add treatment details in public. See "Responding" below.

### Who to ask and when
- **Who:** patients who completed a veneer case or smile makeover and seemed genuinely satisfied at delivery. Veneer cases are the priority because their reviews tend to mention veneers naturally.
- **When:** within **24–48 hours** of the final seat/delivery visit. One ask. One gentle reminder a few days later is acceptable; do not pursue beyond that.
- **How:** the front desk sends a short SMS or email with a **direct Google review link** (use the GBP "Get more reviews" short link). Make it one tap.

### Message templates (on-voice, ready to send)

**SMS — after a veneer case**
> Hi [First name], this is the team at Exquisite Dentistry. It was good to see you. If you have a moment, a short review of your experience helps others find us. Here is the link: [Google review link]. No need to reply. Thank you.

**Email — after a veneer case**
> Subject: A quick note from Exquisite Dentistry
>
> Hi [First name],
>
> Thank you for trusting Dr. Aguil with your care. If you would like to share a few words about your experience, you can leave a review here: [Google review link].
>
> Writing about the work you had done, in your own words, is genuinely helpful to others who are deciding. Only if you have the time.
>
> Warm regards,
> The team at Exquisite Dentistry
> (323) 272-2388

**SMS — gentle one-time reminder (optional, a few days later)**
> Hi [First name], following up once in case it is useful — here is the review link if you would like to share your experience: [Google review link]. Either way, thank you for visiting.

Notes: the templates do not tell the patient what to say or steer toward five stars. That keeps them policy-safe and lets veneer mentions happen naturally.

### Responding to reviews

Respond to **every** review, positive and negative, in voice, within a few days. Sign as the practice, not a named individual.

**Positive — keep it plain, do not confirm treatment**
> Thank you for taking the time to write this. We are glad you had a good experience with us. — Exquisite Dentistry

Do not reply with anything like "we're so happy your veneers turned out great," which confirms the patient is a patient and what they had done — a HIPAA exposure even when they disclosed it first. Keep your side neutral.

**Negative — calm, take it offline, no detail**
> Thank you for the feedback. We take this seriously and would like to understand what happened. Please call us at (323) 272-2388 and ask for the office manager. — Exquisite Dentistry

- Never confirm the person is a patient, never reference any treatment, never argue.
- Move the substance to a phone call.
- If a review is fake or violates Google policy, respond calmly and **also** flag it to Google for removal.

## 4. NAP and citations

Conflicting NAP data is the most common quiet drag on local rankings. Standardize one string and make every listing match it exactly.

### The canonical NAP string

Use this everywhere, character for character:

```
Exquisite Dentistry
6227 Wilshire Blvd
Los Angeles, CA 90048
(323) 272-2388
```

Decisions baked into this string:
- **"Blvd" with no trailing period.** Resolve the "Blvd" vs "Blvd." inconsistency in favor of **no period**, and match it on the website footer, schema, GBP, and every directory. The mismatch itself is the problem; eliminate it.
- **Phone format:** `(323) 272-2388` for display; the underlying tel: link can be `+13232722388`.
- **Suite/unit:** if the office has a suite number, include it the same way in every listing or omit it the same way in every listing.
- **Business name:** exactly `Exquisite Dentistry` — no "Dr. Aguil," no "Cosmetic Dentistry LA," no neighborhood add-ons.

### Directories to check and align

Audit each of these and correct any NAP that does not match the canonical string. Claim the listing where possible.

- **Google Business Profile** — the anchor; everything else matches this.
- **Yelp** — high priority (see Section 5).
- **Apple Maps (Apple Business Connect)** — claim and verify; powers iPhone Maps and Siri. Often overlooked.
- **Healthgrades** — claim Dr. Aguil's provider profile; align practice NAP.
- **AACD "Find a Cosmetic Dentist" directory** — only if Dr. Aguil is an AACD member. If he is, confirm the listing and align NAP; membership is also a credibility signal worth surfacing on the site. If not a member, skip (do not list falsely).
- **Bing Places** — claim and align.
- **Vitals, Zocdoc, RateMDs** — if profiles exist (often auto-created), claim and correct.
- **Facebook / Instagram** — business profile address and phone must match.
- **Data aggregators** — check **Data Axle, Localeze/Neustar, Foursquare**; these feed many smaller directories, so fixing them propagates.
- **The practice website** — footer, contact page, and **LocalBusiness/Dentist schema** (`name`, `address`, `telephone`) must match the canonical string exactly.

Practical method: build a small spreadsheet listing each directory, the current NAP found, and a fixed/verified checkbox. Re-audit quarterly — listings drift over time.

## 5. Yelp

Yelp matters for these specific terms for two reasons:
1. Yelp's own **"Best Veneers in Los Angeles" listicle pages rank organically** on Google for veneers searches, so a strong Yelp presence can earn a second front-page slot.
2. Many LA patients search **inside Yelp** directly when choosing cosmetic providers.

### What to do
- **Claim and complete the Yelp Business page:** canonical NAP, hours, real photos (including consented before/afters where Yelp allows), categories (Cosmetic Dentists, General Dentistry), services, financing/insurance details.
- **Keep it active:** add photos and respond to reviews.

### The policy line on reviews — important
Yelp **discourages directly soliciting reviews** and actively filters reviews it believes were requested. Do not text or email patients asking for a Yelp review, and do not use review-request software to push patients to Yelp.

**Compliant alternative — make Yelp visible, let reviews come organically:**
- Add a tasteful **"Find us on Yelp" link/badge** on the website and in the office (passive display is allowed, direct solicitation is not).
- **Respond** to the reviews that arrive (calmly, in voice, with the same HIPAA discipline as Section 3).
- Concentrate the active **review-request effort on Google** (Section 3), where soliciting is allowed, and let Yelp accrue naturally.

## 6. Measurement

Track a small, consistent set of numbers monthly. Same day each month, same person, same sheet.

### Pull from GBP Insights (Performance) each month
- **Calls** from the profile.
- **Direction requests.**
- **Website clicks.**
- **Bookings / messages** (if enabled).
- **Search queries** that surfaced the profile — watch for "veneers," "veneers los angeles," neighborhood variants, and "cosmetic dentist."

### Rank tracking
- **Map-pack position for "veneers los angeles"** — check from a location near the office (rank varies by searcher location; note where you checked, or use a local-rank tool).
- **Neighborhood variants:** "veneers beverly hills," "veneers west hollywood," "veneers mid wilshire," "porcelain veneers los angeles," "smile makeover los angeles."
- Record both **map-pack** and **organic/Yelp-listicle** presence for each term.

### Reviews
- **Total Google review count** and **average rating**, logged monthly to show **velocity** (reviews added per month).
- **Count of reviews that mention "veneers"** or "smile makeover."
- Yelp review count/rating, tracked but not chased.

### Cadence
- **Monthly:** log everything above (30 minutes). Note what changed since last month.
- **Quarterly:** re-audit NAP across directories (Section 4); review which GBP posts/photos correlated with movement.
- **Ongoing:** keep the review workflow running every week — it is the engine behind most of the monthly movement.

### What good looks like over 3–6 months
- Steady monthly review growth with veneers mentioned organically.
- Rising "veneers"-related search queries in GBP Insights.
- Map-pack appearance for "veneers los angeles" from nearby, and stronger pack rank for the neighborhood variants where proximity is on your side.
- Consistent NAP everywhere, with no new drift at the quarterly audit.
